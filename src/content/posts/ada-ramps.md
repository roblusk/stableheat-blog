ADA ramps occupy a strange space in commercial property management: they're legally required, used by the most vulnerable customers, and sit at the exact place where ice forms first and lasts longest. A bad winter morning at the wrong entry can produce the kind of slip-and-fall claim that no facility manager wants to read about in a deposition.

Heated ramps solve the problem at the source.

## Why they're the right place to start

Snowmelt is overkill for some surfaces and absolutely justified for others. ADA ramps land squarely in the second category for three reasons:

1. **Liability concentration.** Falls on accessibility infrastructure carry both ADA-compliance scrutiny and standard premises-liability exposure.
2. **User vulnerability.** Wheelchair users, walker users, and elderly customers have the lowest tolerance for an iced surface and the highest injury risk from a fall.
3. **Surface area.** Most ADA ramps are small — 100 to 300 square feet — which keeps both the install cost and the operating cost manageable.

A StableHeat snowmelt installation on a ramp typically runs $2,000-$5,000 depending on length and slope. A single slip-and-fall claim can run twenty times that, before legal fees.

> The math doesn't require optimism. Even one prevented incident pays for the system several times over.

## The properties leading the move

We're seeing snowmelt on ADA ramps standardized across:

- **Hospitals and outpatient clinics**, where the patient population is by definition vulnerable
- **Pharmacies and grocery chains**, where elderly foot traffic peaks in winter
- **Banks and credit unions**, where branch entries are heavily used by senior customers
- **Senior living facilities**, where the math is so obvious it almost doesn't need a memo

For new construction, embedding the elements in the ramp pour adds a few hundred dollars of incremental material on a project already specified for compliance. For existing ramps, surface or near-surface retrofits work cleanly during a routine slab refresh.

The compliance reason got these ramps built. The comfort and safety reason is what should keep them clear.
