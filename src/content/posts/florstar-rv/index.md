The RV industry has spent decades treating heating as an afterthought. The standard approach — a propane furnace blower in a tight enclosed space — works, in the sense that the cabin doesn't freeze. It also wakes occupants every cycle, dries the air aggressively, drains propane reserves on cold nights, and leaves the floor itself uncomfortably cold.

A small number of manufacturers have decided this isn't acceptable. Florstar is one of them.

## What integrating radiant during manufacturing changes

When a heating system gets specified during the build rather than retrofitted later, several things become possible:

1. **Element layout matches the floor plan exactly.** No working around cabinetry, no compromised coverage.
2. **Wiring runs in the build, not over it.** Lower visual impact, cleaner installation.
3. **Controller placement is intentional.** The thermostat sits where it should, not wherever it could.
4. **Power integration is designed in.** The unit's electrical system anticipates the heating load.

For Florstar specifically, our radiant element gets laid across the interior subfloor as part of the build sequence — the element panels are positioned, the leads are run, and the finish flooring goes down on top. By the time the unit hits the dealer, the heating is invisible and operational.

> The best RV heating system is the one the buyer doesn't have to know about.

## Why RVs benefit disproportionately

A few thousand square feet of residential floor area can absorb a lot of forced-air heating without the occupants noticing. A few hundred square feet of RV interior cannot. Every imperfection in the heating system gets amplified by the small space:

- Blower noise is more disruptive
- Air dryness builds faster
- Cold floor zones are unavoidable underfoot
- Propane consumption directly affects how long you can boondock

Radiant solves all four at once. The blower becomes a backup. The air stays at natural humidity. The floor is uniform temperature edge to edge. The propane lasts longer because the furnace runs less.

For full-time RVers — the customers who notice these things daily — the difference is decisive.

## What a Florstar install looks like in practice

The Florstar Pictures folder we maintain documents motor home installations with the element panels visible across the interior floor area. The strips run parallel through the main living space, with leads routed to the controller. After finish-floor installation, the system is invisible and operational from day one.

Owners describe the difference in consistent terms: warmer mornings, quieter nights, and an end to the propane-management calculus that defines winter RV life. That's a fair summary of what radiant brings to the recreational vehicle market.

For RV buyers evaluating their next purchase: ask whether the unit has integrated radiant heat, or whether the dealer is willing to factory-spec it. The unit that comes with it ready to run is the unit you'll prefer in February.
