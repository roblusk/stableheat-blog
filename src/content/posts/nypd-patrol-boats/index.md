The boats that patrol New York Harbor through January are working in some of the harshest marine winter conditions in the United States. Wind whipping off the Atlantic, salt spray that turns to ice on impact, and crews that need to remain not just functional but *attentive* across eight-to-twelve-hour shifts. The cold isn't a comfort issue. It's an operational issue.

That's the context for one of our longest-running marine partnerships: a decade-plus of shipbuilding work outfitting NYPD patrol boats with self-regulating radiant heating systems.

## Three contracts, three vessel classes

We've now equipped three separate generations of NYPD harbor vessels through partnerships with two specialty shipbuilders:

- **Moose Boats NYPD 1503**: a single high-speed patrol boat built for harbor pursuit operations
- **Gladding-Hearn NYPD 61 Class** (Order 120826): a fleet of patrol boats sized for routine harbor operations
- **Gladding-Hearn NYPD 70 Class** (Order 140676): the latest generation, with expanded mission capability

Three separate AFEs across three classes of vessel signals something specific: the prior installations performed well enough that the procurement teams kept specifying the same heating approach when they could have switched. In marine work, that kind of through-line is rare.

> Procurement repeats are the truest review you can get.

## Why radiant fits patrol-boat operations

Patrol boats face the same heating challenges as any cold-water vessel, and a few that are specific to law enforcement work:

- **Quiet matters.** Surveillance and pursuit operations require crew communications without the background noise of a forced-air blower.
- **Below-deck spaces stay dry.** Officers transition in and out of cold weather and need warm surfaces to dry on. Radiant accomplishes this without injecting more humid air into the cabin.
- **Battery management is critical.** Patrol vessels run on shore power dockside but operate on hours of continuous battery and generator load. Self-regulating elements draw predictably and don't surge.
- **Reliability is non-optional.** A failed heating system on a patrol boat in February isn't a discomfort. It's an officer-safety issue.

## What it represents for our marine work

The NYPD installations have become a reference point we cite to other municipal and federal marine procurement teams. If the heating approach works for NYC's harbor patrol through a Northeast winter, it tends to work for everyone else. The contracts also prove out the long-term durability case for self-regulating elements in salt environments. These systems have been operating across years of harbor service without notable maintenance events.

Three contracts, three classes, one system. That's the marine track record we point to when prospective fleet buyers ask the durability question.
