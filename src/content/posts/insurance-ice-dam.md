Homeowners insurance is, at its core, a numbers business. Carriers have decades of claims data, and when a particular failure mode starts spiking, they react — first with rate increases, then with policy questionnaires, then with mitigation requirements.

Ice dams are now firmly in the second phase of that cycle.

## What's changing in renewal forms

In several northern-tier states, major carriers have added new questions to homeowner renewal documents over the last two cycles:

- "Has your roof had ice dam formation in the last five years?"
- "Do you have a roof or gutter de-icing system installed?"
- "Have you replaced your roof in the last ten years? If so, what underlayment was used?"

These aren't idle questions. They're informing both pricing and underwriting decisions. Properties with a documented ice-dam history are increasingly being non-renewed in some markets, especially in the Upper Midwest, New England, and the Mountain West.

> The carrier reads "ice dam in 2023" and adjusts the premium. Sometimes more than that.

## Mitigation credits are starting to appear

The flip side of the same story: a handful of carriers are now offering modest premium credits for properties with installed roof de-icing systems, similar to the credits that have existed for storm shutters in coastal markets and reinforced roofs in tornado country.

The discounts aren't large yet — typically $50-$200 per year — but they're trending in the same direction shutter and impact-resistant glass discounts trended fifteen years ago. Mitigation gets recognized once enough properties have it installed for the actuaries to model it.

## Why this matters for upgrade decisions

For any homeowner in an ice-dam-prone region considering a roof replacement, this is the moment to install a self-regulating de-icing system as part of the project. You get:

- Lower lifetime risk of a $20,000-$80,000 ice dam claim (the average claim has roughly tripled in the last decade)
- A favorable answer to the renewal questionnaire
- Eligibility for the mitigation credit as it rolls out across more carriers
- A documented before/after that improves resale appraisal

The carrier is already asking the question. The cheaper answer is to fix the problem before it shows up on a claim form.
