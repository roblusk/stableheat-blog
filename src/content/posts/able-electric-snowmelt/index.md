They went through licensed electricians like Able Electric — the kind of contractor who already knows the homeowner, knows the panel, and knows which inspector covers the township. Able handles the trade-side work: transformers, line-voltage runs, code compliance, the load calculation, the final connection. We supply the elements, the controls, and the engineering. ## What "complete" means at this house

The Able install on this property covers both ends of the winter problem at a residential home:

- **Driveway and walkway snowmelt** at ground level, so the homeowner doesn't shovel out at 5:30 a.m. before the morning commute
- **Roof de-icing** along the eaves and valleys, so meltwater off the warm parts of the roof doesn't refreeze into ice dams at the cold edges

Treating those as one project — one contractor, one control philosophy, one walk-through at handover — produces a different result than treating them as two. The driveway sensor and the roof sensor share commissioning notes. The transformer sizing accounts for both loads. If the homeowner ever has a question, there's one phone number to call.

## Why the licensed-electrician model works

Snowmelt and roof heat involve real electrical loads. A medium driveway zone can pull tens of amps; a multi-zone install with roof and ramp can require its own subpanel and a step-down transformer. That work belongs to a licensed electrician, full stop. When we ship through Able and partners like them, the install gets:

1. **Code compliance signed by a name on the local permit** — not a manufacturer's rep doing a courtesy walk-through.
2. **Proper conductor sizing and overcurrent protection** — calculated for the actual install, not assumed from a generic spec sheet.
3. **A service relationship that survives the warranty.** The electrician who ran the conduit is the electrician you call when a sensor needs replacing in year seven.

We've watched too many heating installs go sideways because the install side was treated as an afterthought. The product can be perfect; if the connections are wrong, the customer's experience is bad. The trade matters.

## What the partnership looks like in practice

Able Electric — and the contractors who work the way they do — handle the sales conversation directly with the homeowner. We support with:

- **Element specification and layout** based on the surface dimensions, climate zone, and slope
- **Transformer and control package sizing** matched to the load
- **Commissioning support** during the first cold-weather firing
- **Documentation** the contractor can hand the homeowner at closeout

The contractor handles everything the customer actually sees: the quote, the schedule, the install crew, the cleanup, the punch list. Our job is to make sure the package shows up correctly specified, fully documented, and ready to install.
