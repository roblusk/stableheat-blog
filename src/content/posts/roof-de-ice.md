Anyone who has owned a house in a snow climate has watched it happen. Snow falls on the roof. Heat from the attic warms the underside of the deck. Snow melts and runs to the eaves, where the roof overhangs unheated air. The meltwater refreezes. The ice builds backward from the eave. Eventually it backs up under the shingles, finds the underlayment, and starts dripping into the master bedroom ceiling.

This is an ice dam. It happens to roofs every winter, in nearly every part of the country that gets sustained sub-freezing weather. And while attic insulation and ventilation are the right long-term answers, they're rarely sufficient — and they aren't options once the snow is already on the roof.

## What roof de-ice actually does

A StableHeat roof de-icing system places self-regulating heating cable along the roof eaves, in valleys, and inside gutters and downspouts. When temperature and moisture sensors detect dam-forming conditions, the cable energizes and creates a continuous melt path: water flows down the eave, through the gutter, and out the downspout instead of freezing back into a dam.

The cable doesn't try to clear the whole roof — it just keeps a path open. The bulk of the snow stays where it is. The damaging ice never forms.

> Stop the dam, and the rest of the winter takes care of itself.

## Why self-regulating matters here

Constant-wattage de-ice cable runs the same draw whether it's covered by snow or sitting in 50-degree spring sun. That's a significant amount of wasted electricity. It's also a fire risk in dry conditions — covered cable has nowhere to dump its heat.

StableHeat's self-regulating cable backs off automatically when it isn't needed. A snow-covered section pulls more current; a sunlit, snow-free section drops to near-idle. You're paying for actual de-icing, not for the cable's habit of running.

## When to install

The best time to install roof de-ice is during a roof replacement, when the eave membrane is exposed and cable can be cleanly integrated. The second-best time is now — surface-mount installations work fine on existing roofs and can usually be done in a single day.

If you've patched a ceiling stain twice in the last five winters, that's the signal. The patch is treating the symptom.
